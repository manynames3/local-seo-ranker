# Sales Walkthrough

Use this walkthrough when showing Local SEO Ranker to a prospect, agency buyer, or technical reviewer.

## Setup

1. Open [https://local-seo-ranker.pages.dev](https://local-seo-ranker.pages.dev).
2. Sign in with the workspace email and invite code.
3. Enter a real business, target keyword, city, state, website, and competitor URLs.
4. Click **Find center** if latitude and longitude are not already known.
5. Choose **Live Maps scan** when observed rank evidence is needed, or **Strategy estimate** for a fast planning read.

## Talk Track

"Local SEO reporting usually jumps straight into generic audits. This starts with the territory. We enter the business, market, keyword, and competitors, then the app shows where the business is winning, where it is close, and where competitors can take the local pack."

"The grid is not just a visual. It feeds into the authority model: topical coverage, entity clarity, service-depth, trust signals, internal links, GBP readiness, and AI-search readiness. The output is a build plan, not just a score."

"Live Maps scans are protected by account credits, scan caps, caching, and server-side provider credentials. Strategy reports are instant and useful for planning, while live scans provide the rank evidence you would use in a client-facing report."

## What To Show

1. The account and credit panel.
2. Find center from a real business and market.
3. Run a 7x7 or 9x9 live Maps scan.
4. Explain the rank grid legend: 1-3 local pack, 4-6 near win, 7-10 contested, 11+ weak zone.
5. Move from weak pockets into the market pulse and strategic read.
6. Show the competitor comparison and the topic/entity/internal-link recommendations.
7. Export the report as copy, JSON, CSV, or print.
8. Show saved scan history and, if signed in as admin, the admin overview.

## Claims To Avoid

- Do not guarantee future Google rankings.
- Do not present strategy-mode cells as observed Maps evidence.
- Do not claim automated billing, scheduled scans, or team invitations are complete yet.
- Do not expose or share provider API keys.
